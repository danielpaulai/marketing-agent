# LinkedIn Post Writing Variations, Purely Personal Reference
# Variation A (Framework-Heavy) + Variation B (Story-Flow)
# by Daniel Paul · Purely Personal

---

## HOW TO CHOOSE

**Variation A, Framework-Heavy:**
Use when the post delivers tactical frameworks, numbered systems, step-by-step processes, or platform commentary. Optimised for saves and reposts. Think training manual, not conversation.

**Variation B, Story-Flow:**
Use when the post builds connection through personal story, transformation, vulnerability, or perspective shift. Optimised for comments and DMs. Think conversation over coffee, not training manual.

**If the CMO calendar specifies intent:** follow it. If not, choose based on topic:
- "How to", "X steps", "stop doing X" → Variation A
- "I learned", "here's what changed", "I used to" → Variation B

---

## VARIATION A, FRAMEWORK-HEAVY

### Intent Options

**EDUCATING**, Teaching tactical frameworks
- Hook: "If I had to [goal] by tomorrow, I would:"
- Structure: 5–12 numbered actions with brief why/how
- Close: Encouragement + signature closing

**ENGAGEMENT**, Platform commentary or contrarian takes
- Hook: "If I was CEO of [Platform]:" or "Stop [common action]. Do this instead:"
- Structure: 5–10 numbered alternatives/changes
- Close: Question to audience

**SOFT SELLING**, Demonstrating methodology through framework
- Hook: "Here's the exact process I use to [outcome]:"
- Structure: 6–10 numbered steps (subtly showcases your method)
- Close: "You have two choices" framework

**HARD SELLING**, Direct offer with benefit breakdown
- Hook: "I'm opening [X] spots. Here's what you get:"
- Structure: 5–8 numbered benefits with specifics
- Close: Clear CTA

---

### Hook Requirements (Variation A)

Must promise a structured, actionable framework.

| Intent | Hook Patterns |
|--------|--------------|
| Educating | "If I had to [specific goal] by tomorrow, I would:" |
| Educating | "Here's my exact [number]-step process for [outcome]:" |
| Engagement | "If I was CEO of [Platform], here's what I'd fix:" |
| Engagement | "Stop [common action]. Do this instead:" |
| Soft Selling | "Here's the exact process I use to [impressive outcome]:" |
| Hard Selling | "I'm opening [number] spots for [specific transformation]." |

**Hook rules:**
- Start with: I, If, Here's, Stop, Want, or [Number]
- Under 12 words
- Promises specific framework or list
- Creates value expectation immediately
- ❌ No storytelling openings, vague promises, or throat-clearing

---

### Post Structure (Variation A)

**Primary (Educating, Engagement, Soft Selling):**
```
[HOOK: Framework promise, under 12 words]
(Save this + Repost if useful ♻️)
[Optional: 1 sentence context or constraint]

1. [Action/insight + brief why/how, 10–18 words]
2. [Action/insight + brief why/how, 10–18 words]
3. [Action/insight + brief why/how, 10–18 words]
4. [Action/insight + brief why/how, 10–18 words]
5. [Action/insight + brief why/how, 10–18 words]

[Continue for 5–12 points total, optimal 7–10]
[Encouragement, 1 sentence]
[Client's signature closing: "Good luck!" or from Voice DNA]
[Optional: "Real talk:" + reframe, 1 sentence]
P.S. [One clear idea, 8–15 words max]
```

**Hard Selling:**
```
[HOOK: Offer announcement, under 12 words]
[What you're offering, 1–2 sentences]
Here's what you get:

1. [Benefit + specific detail, 10–15 words]
2. [Benefit + specific detail, 10–15 words]
[Continue for 5–8 benefits]

[Social proof, 1 sentence]
[Clear CTA: "DM me [WORD]" or "Link in comments"]
P.S. [Urgency, deadline, or qualifier, 8–15 words max]
```

---

### Writing Standards (Variation A)

**Length:** 1,300–1,500 characters exactly

**List structure (critical):**
- Number every point (1. 2. 3.)
- Keep points similar length (10–18 words per point)
- Each point must be independently valuable
- Action-first OR insight-first (stay consistent throughout)
- No filler points, every point earns its place

**Point writing formula:**
- Action-first: "Install Loom → Record 3 sales calls → Analyse pitch gaps"
- Insight-first: "Voice trains AI better than prompts → Extract 15K words of your writing"
- Add brief why/how: "X because Y" or "X → Y outcome"

**Engagement elements:**
- "(Save this + Repost if useful ♻️)" after hook for EDUCATING posts
- "Good luck!" or client's signature closing
- Optional: "Real talk:" reframe before P.S.
- P.S. with question, context, or achievement

**Variation A forbidden patterns:**
- ❌ Don't start every point identically
- ❌ Don't make points progressively longer
- ❌ Don't save the best for last, start strong
- ❌ "Let's dive in" after hook
- ❌ "Game-changer" / "Revolutionary"
- ❌ Generic advice without specifics

## VARIATION B, STORY-FLOW

### Intent Options

**NURTURING**, Building connection through personal story
- Hook: Personal moment or admission
- Flow: Setup → Story → Lesson → Application
- Close: Relatable question or supportive statement

**SOFT SELLING**, Transformation story with choice framework
- Hook: Achievement or unexpected outcome
- Flow: Before → Problem → What changed → Result → Two choices
- Close: Supportive offer

**ENGAGEMENT**, Perspective shift through experience
- Hook: Controversial statement or quoted criticism
- Flow: Acknowledge → Story/experience → Reframe → Question
- Close: Direct question to audience

**EDUCATING**, Teaching through story/insight
- Hook: Discovery or surprising lesson
- Flow: Problem → What I learned → Why it matters → How to apply
- Close: Simple encouragement

---

### Hook Requirements (Variation B)

Must feel like the opening of a conversation, not a headline.

| Intent | Hook Patterns |
|--------|--------------|
| Nurturing | "I finally [significant moment]." |
| Nurturing | "I used to believe [X]. I was wrong." |
| Soft Selling | "[Unexpected outcome] happened yesterday." |
| Soft Selling | "Three [time period] ago, [starting situation]." |
| Engagement | "[Quote of criticism or pushback]" |
| Educating | "Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]:" |

**Hook rules:**
- Start with: I, You, If, When, [Quote], or [Number]
- Under 12 words
- Feels like natural speech, not a headline
- Creates curiosity about what happened next
- ❌ No generic questions, corporate speak, or invented moments

---

### Post Structures (Variation B)

**NURTURING, Personal Story + Lesson:**
```
[HOOK: Personal moment or admission]

[Setup: Where/when/context, 1–2 sentences]

[What happened, 2–3 short paragraphs]

[The turning point or realisation, short paragraph]

[The lesson learned, short paragraph]

[How this applies to reader, 1–2 sentences]

[Question to audience]

[Client's signature closing]

P.S. [One clear idea, 8–15 words max]
```

**SOFT SELLING, Transformation + Two Choices:**
```
[HOOK: Achievement or unexpected outcome]

[The before: Where I/they started, 1–2 sentences]

[The problem, short paragraph]

[What changed, short paragraph]

[The result, short paragraph]

I'll keep this short.

You have two choices:

1. [Specific action path with timeline and outcome, 2–3 sentences]

2. Don't.

[Supportive close]

P.S. [Social proof, urgency, or context, 8–15 words max]
```

**ENGAGEMENT, Controversial Response:**
```
[HOOK: Quote of criticism or controversial statement]

[Acknowledge it, 1 sentence]

[Why this happens or why people think this, short paragraph]

[Your experience or perspective, short paragraph]

[The reframe or truth, short paragraph]

[What to do instead or perspective shift, 1–2 sentences]

[Question to audience]

[Client's signature closing]

P.S. [Context or recent win, 8–15 words max]
```

**EDUCATING, Story-Based Teaching:**
```
[HOOK: Discovery or surprising insight]

[Setup: What led to this insight, 1–2 sentences]

[The problem everyone faces, short paragraph]

[What I learned/discovered, short paragraph]

[Why this matters, short paragraph]

[What to do instead, 2–3 short sentences]

[Simple encouragement]

[Client's signature closing]

P.S. [One clear idea, 8–15 words max]
```

---

### Writing Standards (Variation B)

**Length:** 1,300–1,500 characters exactly

**Paragraph structure (critical):**
- Ultra-short paragraphs (1–3 sentences max)
- Frequent line breaks, white space is readability
- Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Never more than 3 sentences without a break

**Sentence rhythm:**
- Mix ultra-short (1–5 words) with medium (8–15 words)
- Use short for: transition, emotion, emphasis
- Use medium for: story, explanation, context
- Examples: "I know." "Real talk:" "Here's the thing:"

**Conversational markers (apply from Voice DNA):**
- "Here's the unexpected part:"
- "Real talk:"
- "Listen..."
- "Oh well..."
- "Anyhow..."

**Variation B forbidden patterns:**
- ❌ "Let me tell you about..."
- ❌ "Today I want to share..."
- ❌ Over-explain emotions ("I felt so devastated...")
- ❌ Telegraph lessons ("Here's what this taught me:")
- ❌ Let the story reveal the insight naturally, don't announce it

## ANTI-HALLUCINATION RULE (BOTH VARIATIONS)

**Never invent information.** Use ONLY:
- Information from the brain dump or reference context
- Client's documented experiences from files
- Generic authority positioning as a last resort (no specific claims)

**Never:**
- Invent client stories or results
- Create fictional case studies or numbers
- Assume client background or credentials
- Fabricate tactics or frameworks not in source material

**Before including any claim:** Is this from the provided source? If NO → delete it.

**Technical:**
- [ ] 1,300–1,500 characters exactly
- [ ] Proper structure for chosen variation
- [ ] Mobile readable (short paragraphs, white space)

**Variation A specific:**
- [ ] 5–12 numbered points (optimal 7–10)
- [ ] Points 10–18 words each, consistent length
- [ ] Each point independently valuable

**Variation B specific:**
- [ ] Ultra-short paragraphs throughout
- [ ] Emotions feel genuine, not manufactured
- [ ] Story flows: before → turning point → lesson → application
- [ ] "Read aloud" test: sounds like the person, not a post
