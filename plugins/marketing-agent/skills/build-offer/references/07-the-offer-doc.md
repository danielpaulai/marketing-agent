# The Offer Doc (the one-page document that sells without a call)

Extracted from the live Offer Engine session. This doc format took 107 readers, turned them into credit-card-in-hand applicants, and filled 20 spots in one week. The big idea behind it:

> "A compelling offer beats a convincing argument every single time." (Dean Jackson)

If the offer is great, even average copy sells stacks of it. If the offer is weak, brilliant copy sells a little. So the doc is simple. The OFFER inside it does the work.

## What the Offer Doc is

A one or two page piece of text, classically a Google Doc, that sells the program by chat or email. No sales call. No funnel. The prospect gets the link in a DM or an email, reads it, and replies with a keyword.

Why a doc and not a fancy page: you can live-update it. Cross out "10 spots" and write "7". That crossed-out number is real scarcity the reader can watch happening.

## The Structure (in order)

### 1. Title + live spot counter
Program name, plus the spots line right at the top: "~~10~~ 7 spots left". Update it as people join.

### 2. The outcome opener
One clear sentence. "Here's what I'm doing with [program] in [month]. The outcome is simple: get you to [measurable result] while [lifestyle constraint]."

Hit up to three levers: **money, impact, time**. The more measurable, the better. ("A million dollars a year, getting incredible client results, working four days a week.")

### 3. Hand-selected exclusivity
"I'm going to hand-select a group of [niche] and help them [big verb] over a [period]." Then one sentence on the mix: 1:1 strategy, a custom game plan, proven systems, project cycles, personal guidance. Loose, not a curriculum.

### 4. The model: Phase 1 / Phase 2
The vibe is **"starts good, gets better."** Long programs back-load their value, so pull a quick win forward:

- **Phase 1 (first 30-60 days): quick wins.** "The first months are very tactical, focused on putting enough money in your bank account to more than pay for the program for the entire year."
- **Phase 2: growth.** The bigger build, the compounding stuff.

Each phase gets its own mini promise.

### 5. What's inside: 5-6 numbered blocks
List the deliverables BUT run every one through **feature → "so you..."**:
"Every six weeks we get together on Zoom to map your next game plan, **so you** keep building momentum."

Never a bare deliverable. Always the "so you / so that" tail.

**Block 6 is the money line:** "It easily pays for itself before you pay anything."

### 6. Pay plan (payment shapes)
Think in shapes: all upfront, easy monthly, or the juicy one: **"No payments for the first two months, then 10 payments of X."**

The math: monthly price × 12 ÷ 10 = the new payment. Same annual money, feels free to start. It answers the two biggest objections in one move: "I'm busy right now" and "I don't have the cash." They pay you out of the money you help them make.

Three cautions (from the session):
- The pay plan total must cost MORE than pay-in-full. People default, admin costs money.
- If you're hurting for cash now, don't do deferred starts. Cash now beats cash in two months.
- A too-generous plan attracts low-caliber buyers. Filter hard.

Renewal bonus move: at year end, drop them back to the normal monthly rate. Same money, feels like a discount.

### 7. Quick facts + the soft guarantee
"After that, we'll go month to month as long as you love it and you're making progress." That one line is a guarantee, a filter, and a retention frame at once.

### 8. Qualifying criteria (small group, best fit)
"It's a small group, so I'm looking for the best fit here. If you're a [niche] doing [number] or more, have [asset], can [commitment]..."

**Rule: bullet #5 is funny.** Something silly ("devastating handsomeness optional") to take the seriousness out. Every list of criteria ends with a wink.

### 9. The CTA: keyword reply
"If that's you and you'd like to join us, DM me the words **'I'M IN'**."

They arrived at this doc from chat or email, so the reply goes back the same way. One keyword. Zero friction. Then: "We'll get you enrolled and book your one-on-one game plan."

### 10. The instant bonus
Last line: "As soon as you decide, you get instant access to [quick-win bonus]." Something fast and valuable the moment they say yes, before onboarding even starts.

## Optional blocks (use when needed)

- **Why this / why now** — "How come when someone joins a coaching program, THEY take all the risk?" / an event or date hook
- **Juxtaposition** — the old dumb way everyone does it vs. your new way (your unique mechanism)
- **Proof** — results, names, credibility lines
- **Add-ons** — "the core offer is X; for an extra Y you can add 1:1 calls"
- **A visual model** — nice, not necessary. Clarity beats decoration.

## The Long-Form Version (the full sales letter)

The structure above is the lean chat-doc. When the offer warrants a full prospectus (a flagship program, a webpage, a Notion page), expand it into a long-form sales letter. This is the default depth for the `offer-doc.html` template and the Notion output. A thin offer doc is a bug. The flow, modeled on a proven long-form page:

1. **Hook headline.** "Let me tell you something nobody tells [niche]."
2. **Problem agitation.** They are the best-kept secret. The market does not know they exist. Weaker competitors win the work simply by showing up.
3. **The real problem.** It is not their skill. They do not have a system.
4. **Founder empathy.** "I did the same thing you are doing, then I built a system." First person, honest, short.
5. **The named mechanism.** Three labelled parts (for example ATTRACT, CONVERT, COMPOUND). Give them names. This is the unique mechanism.
6. **Why this works when everything else has not.** Implementation, not information. Credibility.
7. **The phased plan.** Each phase: an italic subtitle, two or three "What you'll build" sub-blocks with bullets, and a "By the end of Phase X" outcome callout.
8. **Everything you get.** A checklist, every item run through "so you...".
9. **Who this is for / Won't work for you if.** Two lists. Qualify in and qualify out.
10. **The investment.** Price plus payment shapes. Highlight pay-in-full.
11. **What waiting actually costs you.** Cost of inaction. Be honest, not hypey.
12. **The guarantee / safety net.**
13. **FAQ.** Five to ten real objections answered plainly.
14. **Enrolment / scarcity.** Real numbers only. "6 spots, 3 taken."
15. **CTA.** One keyword reply.
16. **The instant bonus.**
17. **P.S. and P.P.S.** Re-state the stakes, then what happens the moment they join.

Red section headings (the one accent doing structural work) organize the page. Everything else stays black on cream. Still no em dashes, ever. The chat-doc structure above is the compressed form of this; both are valid depending on how the prospect arrives.

## The Test Before Sending

Read the doc top to bottom and ask: does the reader know exactly **what they get, what it costs, what happens if it doesn't work, why now, and what to do next** without ever needing a call? If any answer is fuzzy, the doc isn't done.
