# Human Writing Standards, Purely Personal
# by Daniel Paul · Purely Personal

---

## THE ONE TEST

Every piece of content passes one test before delivery:

**Would Daniel Paul read this and say "I wrote that"?**

If no, rewrite. No exceptions.

---

## THE INVISIBLE DIAGNOSTIC (score every post 0–4)

Daniel's standard for post quality. Every post is scored internally before delivery.

**Dimension 1, VOICE (0 or 1)**
Does this sound like a specific human wrote it?
1 = yes, this has a distinct personality.
0 = could have been written by any AI or any generic content person.

**Dimension 2, POV (0 or 1)**
Does this take a clear position?
1 = there is a specific opinion, stance, or conviction in this post.
0 = it describes a situation without saying anything meaningful about it.

**Dimension 3, SPECIFICITY (0 or 1)**
Is there at least one detail that could not have been invented?
1 = specific number, specific person, specific timeframe, specific result, specific place.
0 = everything is vague enough to apply to anyone anywhere.

**Dimension 4, ASKING (0 or 1)**
Does this post ask the reader to do something or feel something?
1 = there is a clear action (DM, comment, save) or emotional resonance (guilt, pride, recognition).
0 = reader finishes reading and nothing was asked of them.

**Target: 4/4 on every post.**
3/4: acceptable only for experimental formats.
2/4 or below: rewrite before delivering.

## STRUCTURAL RULES

**Paragraphs**
- Maximum 3 lines per paragraph in LinkedIn posts
- One idea per paragraph
- White space is not wasted space, it's emphasis

**Sentences**
- Short sentences for emphasis. Longer sentences for context.
- Never three long sentences in a row, break them up
- Kill weasel words: "kind of", "sort of", "a bit", "somewhat", "quite"

**Posts**
- LinkedIn: 1,100–1,500 characters (maximum impact zone)
- Hook: the first line must stop the scroll, one job only
- P.S.: always present, always the CTA or most important line

## THE AI PATTERN BLACKLIST

These patterns flag a piece as AI-generated. Kill every one before delivery.

**Structural patterns:**
- "Here are [N] ways to..." as an opener
- "In conclusion..." or "In summary..."
- Bullet points as the primary content format (prose beats bullets)
- Three consecutive sentences all starting with "You"
- Starting with "As a [job title]..."
- Ending with "What do you think? Let me know in the comments!"

**Word patterns:**
- Any word from the banned list (see Voice DNA)
- Exclamation marks used for enthusiasm rather than irony
- "That being said" / "With that in mind" / "Moving forward"
- "It's important to note" / "Worth mentioning"
- Capitalising abstract nouns for no reason (The Journey, The Process)

**Tone patterns:**
- Overly balanced ("On one hand... on the other hand...")
- Inspirational without specificity ("You can do it if you believe")
- Explaining the hook before earning the reader's trust

## HOOK QUALITY TEST

Before finalising any hook, answer:

1. **Does it stop the scroll?** (yes/no, not "probably")
2. **Does it create a gap** the reader must close to understand the post?
3. **Is it under 12 words?** (target under 10)
4. **Does it contain a specific detail**, number, name, timeframe, result?
5. **Would the ICP read this and think "this is about me"?**

If any answer is no, rewrite the hook. The body does not matter if the hook fails.
