# The Offer Engine — The Frame Everything Bolts Onto

> "A compelling offer beats a convincing argument every single time." — Dean Jackson

The **Offer Engine** is the frame of the offer. Like a car's frame, it's the structure every other part bolts onto — engine, wheels, body. Your offer works the same way. Get the frame right and everything else (your marketing, your sales calls, your follow-up) bolts on and runs. Get it wrong and no amount of clever marketing saves you.

## The Job of the Offer

The offer's #1 job is to **overcome objections BEFORE the sales conversation happens.**

If you're grinding through sales calls — handling the same objections over and over, chasing people who "want to think about it" — the offer isn't good enough yet. A great offer can be sold by chat, by an offer doc, sometimes without a call at all. The offer does the heavy lifting so you don't have to.

When you find yourself *convincing*, stop and fix the offer.

## The Risk:Reward Seesaw™

Every buying decision is a seesaw in the prospect's mind:

```
        REWARD  <-------- [ fulcrum ] --------> RISK
     (do I want it?)                      (what could go wrong?)
```

- **REWARD side** — Is the outcome clear? Is it desirable? Can I see it happening to me?
- **RISK side** — Is it expensive? Will it take too long? Will it actually work? Have I been burned before?

The prospect says **yes** the moment **reward outweighs risk** by enough to act *now*.

Our three moves:
1. **Stack the reward** heavier → Promise + Bonuses
2. **Strip the risk** lighter → Guarantee + Payment Plan
3. **Add a nudge** at the fulcrum → Scarcity & Urgency

That's the whole game.

## The Black Box / Skittles Analogy

If someone hands you a sealed black box with a hole in the side and says *"put your hand in,"* you won't. Unknown risk.

Now make the box **transparent**, fill it with **Skittles**, and add: *"if you don't love the Skittles, I'll buy them back at full price AND give you $10 for tacos."*

Now you'd be stupid NOT to put your hand in. Same box. We just made the reward visible and the risk disappear. **That is offer design.**

## The 5 Facets of the Offer Diamond™

The Engine carries the **Offer Diamond** — 5 facets, mapped to the seesaw:

| # | Facet | Seesaw Side | Job |
|---|-------|-------------|-----|
| 1 | **Promise** | Reward | The biggest needle mover. Specific result + set timeframe. |
| 2 | **Bonuses** | Reward | Overcome objections, add speed, reduce effort, add intimacy. |
| 3 | **Guarantee** | Risk | Make it safe to start. Remove or reverse the risk. |
| 4 | **Payment Plan** | Risk | Take money off the table — change terms, not amount. |
| 5 | **Scarcity & Urgency** | Fulcrum | A real reason to act NOW. |

**Promise and Guarantee are the two biggest levers.** Get those two genuinely right and the offer works. The other three are amplifiers — important, but they multiply a strong Promise/Guarantee rather than rescue a weak one.

## Conversion Confidence

People buy **certainty**. The failure pattern: the prospect is crystal clear on the COST but fuzzy on the VALUE. That gap is always a "no."

Our job across all 5 facets is to manufacture **conversion confidence** — the confidence a prospect needs to commit to themselves, to their future, and to you. Every facet either adds confidence or it's dead weight.

## The Two Golden Rules That Run Through Everything

1. **Sell the destination, not the journey.** Hawaii — warm sand, tropical breeze — not the airport security line. Never sell calls, modules, portals, or hours. Sell the outcome.
2. **Make it real.** No fake scarcity, no weasel-clause guarantees, no hype promises. Every element must be something you can actually point to, actually keep, actually mean. Real is leverage; fake is manipulation and prospects can smell it.

## How to Use the Method

1. **Intake** — Gather the raw client information (who they sell to, what they sell, the result, the price, capacity, dates). See the `offer-intake` skill.
2. **Build each facet** — Work the 5 facets in order, reading each reference before you build it: `01-promise` → `05-scarcity-urgency`.
3. **Score** — Traffic-light each facet 🟢🟡🔴. See the `offer-scorer` skill.
4. **Assemble** — Pull the 5 facets into one Offer Diamond.
5. **Output** — Generate the visuals + documents. See the `offer-visualizer` skill.

The order matters: you can't strip risk on a promise that doesn't exist yet. Build reward first, then risk, then the nudge.
