# Headline Frameworks, Purely Personal Reference

## What Makes a World-Class LinkedIn Headline

Based on analysis of top LinkedIn creators (Norbert Bezzina, Jasmin Alic, Lara Acosta, Charlie Hills, Daniel Paul, Hatice Kamran):

The headline has one job: make the right person feel like this profile was written for them. Not for everyone. For them specifically.

Under 220 characters. Every word earns its place.

---

## 10 Proven Headline Patterns (with real examples)

### 1. Ultra-Simple Outcome Statement
Strip everything to one clear sentence. No separators, no lists, no credentials.

Real example: "I help you win on LinkedIn with AI and systems." (Norbert Bezzina)
Why it works: Direct, specific benefit. Reader knows who it's for and what they get immediately.

Formula: I help [ICP] [achieve specific outcome] with [method or differentiator].

---

### 2. The Authority Stack
Lead with the biggest credibility signal first, then name the outcome.

Real example: "Award-winning personal branding and business coach | Founder of Link Up community (in 70+ countries) | Teaching you how to build a trusted online brand and profitable business." (Jasmin Alic)
Why it works: The award and community scale do the heavy lifting before any promise is made.

Formula: [Biggest credential or award] | [Role or creation] | [Outcome for the reader]

---

### 3. The Transformation Timeline
Show the reader you've been where they are and made it out.

Context (Lara Acosta): From unemployed in 2022 to #1 female creator on LinkedIn in 2023.
Why it works: Specific trajectory makes the transformation feel real and achievable.

Formula: From [starting point] to [result] in [timeframe]. Now [what I do for others].

---

### 4. Specific Outcome + Contrast Differentiator
Name exactly what you deliver, then contrast with what you don't.

Real example: "I help founders turn their content into paying customers | Scripts and strategies that sell, not just engage."
Why it works: "Paying customers" is the outcome. "Not just engage" separates from every other content person.

Formula: I help [ICP] [specific outcome] | [Differentiating method or contrast statement]

---

### 5. The Confident Outcome
Bold statement that signals belief and differentiation simultaneously.

Real example: "Personal branding that moves the needle online and offline." (Lara Acosta)
Why it works: No hedging. The contrast phrase makes it specific. Reads like someone who knows what they're doing.

Formula: [What you do] that [specific, differentiated outcome]. No qualifiers.

---

### 6. The Corporate-to-Personal Bridge
Connect the day job with the personal brand expertise for a 3D impression.

Formula: [Corporate role at Recognisable Company] | [Personal authority signal] | [Outcome for the reader]

---

### 7. The Buzzword Swap
Replace job titles with the specific outcome the ICP actually cares about.

Bad: "Marketing Manager"
Good: "I build the demand gen systems that fill B2B sales pipelines."

Rule: Buyers search for the result they need, not the person's job title.

---

### 8. The Thought Leadership Frame
Anchor the headline to a specific idea or philosophy rather than a role.

Formula: [The named idea or system you stand for] | [Outcome it produces for the ICP]

---

### 9. The Calculated Risk
One unexpected element stops the scroll. One only.

Formula: One sharp, unexpected opening line. Never more than one emoji. No cliches.

---

### 10. The Shift of Focus
Move attention entirely from who you are to what the reader gets.

Formula: Write the headline from the reader's perspective entirely. Not what you do, what they receive.

---

## Non-Negotiable Rules

Zero job titles unless they add credibility (Former Google adds context; Marketing Specialist adds nothing).
Zero vague claims: "passionate about," "dedicated to," "helping people grow."
Zero pipe-separated lists of 5 things. Pick 2 maximum.
Use the language the ICP uses to describe their problem, not industry jargon.
If the headline applies to 1,000 other people in the same industry, rewrite it.
The first 8 words appear in search results and notifications. Make them count.
Mobile shows roughly the first 80 to 100 characters before truncation.

## Anti-Patterns to Eliminate

"Helping brands grow with digital marketing"
"Passionate about people and leadership"
"Content creator | Speaker | Coach"
"Transforming businesses through innovation"
"Empowering professionals to reach their full potential"
Any headline ending in "Let's connect!"
