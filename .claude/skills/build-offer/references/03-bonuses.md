# The Bonus Builder™

## Position on the Seesaw
Bonuses sit on the **REWARD** side. They supplement the promise by making success easier, faster, and more likely.

## The Three Bonus Angles

Every bonus should do at least one of these:

1. **Overcome an Objection** — Handle the fear/doubt that stops them buying
2. **Accelerate Speed** — Help them get results faster
3. **Add Ease** — Reduce effort and sacrifice (get closer to "take the pill and wake up attractive")

Plus a fourth angle:
4. **Intimacy/Access** — Get closer to you (1:1 time, small group, personal attention)

## The Value Equation (Complementary Framework)

This pairs with the famous value equation:

> Offer Value = (Desirability of Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Effort Required × Sacrifice Required)

Bonuses increase the top (desirability + likelihood) and decrease the bottom (effort + sacrifice).

## Bonus Types with Examples

### Objection Overcomers
**Example: Joanna Martin** — Teaches selling from stage. Biggest objection: fear of public speaking. Bonus: 8-minute guided visualization meditation that evaporates the fear. Handles the #1 objection before it becomes a reason to say no.

**How to find yours**: List the top 3 objections people have before buying. For each one, create a bonus that dissolves it.

### Speed Accelerators
**Example: Vanessa's Webinar Funnel** — Clients weren't finishing their webinar outlines. Bonus: "If you outline your webinar by Week 3, we'll build the entire funnel for you." Performance-based, time-gated, and it got people moving fast.

**How to find yours**: What's the #1 bottleneck where people get stuck? Create a done-for-you or template that eliminates it.

### Ease Makers (Done-For-You / Plug-and-Play)
The closer you can get to done-for-you, the better. Templates, scripts, swipe files, pre-built systems.

Examples:
- Pre-built funnel templates (just edit the headline)
- Email sequences already written
- Campaign templates ready to deploy
- Scripts and slide decks
- Software tools (CRM, website builder, etc.)

### Intimacy/Access Bonuses
- 1:1 onboarding call
- Small group hot seat session
- VIP day or intensive
- Direct message access for 30 days

These are high perceived value + sales opportunities (the fastest buyers are also fastest implementers and fastest upgraders).

## The "Extract and Repackage" Move

Your bonuses don't need to be NEW things. Often the best bonuses are things you already include in your program that you:
1. Take out
2. Repackage with a name
3. Add back as a "bonus"

This increases perceived value of the same deliverables. "Plus you also get our Onboarding Accelerator call and the Quick-Start Template Pack" sounds much better than just including them silently.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Kitchen Sink / Thud Factor
Old-school thinking: "My program weighs 3 kilos when you drop it on your desk." That's thud factor. Don't add stuff — add VALUE. Every bonus must be congruent with the promise and help people win.

> "Idiots weigh value by the pound." — Gary Halbert

### Mistake 2: Random Bonuses
If your promise is "10 clients in 10 weeks" and your bonus is a meditation app, that's not congruent. Every bonus must map to the promise or address a real objection.

### Mistake 3: Not Thinking Through the Lenses
Don't just brainstorm random things. Use the 4 angles systematically:
1. What objections do I need to overcome?
2. How can I help them go faster?
3. How can I make it easier?
4. Can I add intimacy/access?

## Fast-Action Bonus Strategy

Bonuses for the first X people who buy:
- "The first 5 get a 1:1 strategy session with me"
- "Join by Friday and get the Done-For-You Setup"
- "First 3 get the VIP onboarding intensive"

This creates urgency (covered in Scarcity module) AND rewards the best buyers.
