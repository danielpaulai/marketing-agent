# The Program Promise™

## Position on the Seesaw
The Promise sits on the **REWARD** side. It's the single biggest needle mover in your offer. If the promise is clear and desirable, people take the leap.

## The Formula
**Specific Result + Set Timeframe = Clear Promise**

Your promise needs to pass three tests:
1. **Can I point to it?** — Is it tangible enough that someone can say "that happened"?
2. **Do they get it?** — Is it clear in plain English, no jargon?
3. **Do they want it?** — Is it desirable to your specific market?

## Size of Promise × Timeframe

Two dimensions to think about:
- **Size**: How big is the outcome?
- **Time**: How quickly will it happen?

Small promise + short time = easy to believe, easy to keep, easy to buy.
Big promise + long time = needs to be broken down.

## The "First, Next, Then" Framework (for longer programs)

If selling a monthly/recurring or 6-12 month program, don't just say "over the next year you'll get X." Break it down:

- **FIRST** (30 days): What's the specific quick win? "Your cravings disappear" / "Your Facebook group is set up" / "Your first 3 clients booked"
- **NEXT** (60-90 days): What momentum builds? "You'll start to lose 1kg per week" / "You'll have a full pipeline"
- **THEN** (6-12 months): What's the big outcome? "Keep the weight off forever" / "The 7-Figure Coach"

## Three Product Names That Nail Promise

These are promises so clear you know exactly what you're buying:
1. **The 4-Hour Work Week** — Clear result, clear timeframe
2. **Thin Thighs in 30 Days** — Specific body part, specific timeframe
3. **5-Hour Energy** — You know what you're getting
4. **Start Your Bastard** (Australian spray for stuck engines) — It does what it says on the tin

Your program promise should be THAT clear.

## The Safety Language: "Is Designed To"

When you can't 100% guarantee a result (because clients have to do the work):
> "The program **is designed to** get you [result] in [timeframe]."

This is accurate. Your program design will get the result. You're not promising they'll follow through, but the design does the job.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Vague Promises
"Make your business better" is not a promise. "10 new clients from leads you've already got" IS.
- How many clients?
- What kind of clients?
- With what strategy?
- In what period of time?

### Mistake 2: Overpromising
Simple, small promises that are easy to believe and easy to keep beat hype every time. People have been hyped up to no end. Small + believable + desirable wins.

If selling a hero program: "Set up your Facebook group in a week" is a great promise. It doesn't have to be "make X amount of money."

### Mistake 3: Selling Deliverables Instead of Outcomes
Wrong: "You get 12 coaching calls, a portal, and 3 events"
Right: "10 new clients in 10 weeks"

Deliverables are the airport security line. The outcome is Hawaii.

### Mistake 4: Selling the Process Instead of the Outcome
Wrong: "First we do a strategic planning session, then weekly coaching, then..."
Right: "You'll wake up with warm sand under your feet and a tropical breeze"

Sell the destination. Not the trip to the airport.

## Key Takeaway
People buy certainty. If the prospect is crystal clear about the cost but NOT crystal clear about the value, that's a no. We need to create **conversion confidence** — the confidence a prospect needs to commit to themselves, their future, and to you.
