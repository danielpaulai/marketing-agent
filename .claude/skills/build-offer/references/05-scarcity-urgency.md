# The Scarcity Engine™ (Scarcity & Urgency)

## Position on the Seesaw
Scarcity & Urgency sit at the **FULCRUM** — the tipping point. They put just enough weight on the seesaw to flip people from "I'll think about it" to "let's get started NOW."

## Two Distinct Levers

**Scarcity = Limited NUMBER (supply)**
"I can only take 5 clients this month."

**Urgency = Limited TIME**
"Our kickoff call is next Tuesday."

They're not the same thing. Use both.

## The De Beers Principle

De Beers has vaults full of diamonds. Millions of them. They artificially limit supply to keep prices high. The lesson: **if you want to increase demand, limit supply.**

You already have built-in limits. You just aren't using them.

## Finding Your Real Scarcity

Ask yourself: "How many clients could I comfortably take on this month without things breaking?"

That number is probably 5, 7, 10, maybe 20. Beyond that, onboarding gets clunky, your time gets squeezed, quality drops.

**That IS your scarcity.** Use it.

Script: "I've got 7 spots available, and 3 are already taken. I'm talking to some other people this week, so the first to act gets priority."

## Finding Your Real Urgency

What's the next thing that happens in your program?

- Kickoff call next Tuesday
- Onboarding session on Friday
- Next intake is August 4th
- This month's training covers [topic they need]
- The challenge starts Monday

If they join NOW, they get access to that thing. If they wait, they miss it.

Script: "We start on Tuesday. If you join now, I can get you set up in advance so you get a head start on everyone else."

## The "Now" Factors

### Scarcity Plays (Limited Number)
- "10 spots in [program name]"
- "The first 5 get [fast-action bonus]"
- "I've got 3 openings this month"
- "I can only work with X people at this level"

### Urgency Plays (Limited Time)
- "We start on Tuesday"
- "Our next intake is [date]"
- "The kickoff call is [date] — if you're not in by then, you'll miss it"
- "This month we're teaching [topic] — that's next week"
- "Enrollment closes Friday"

### Combined
- "I've got 5 spots left and we start Tuesday" — both levers at once

## Fast-Action Bonuses

Tie bonuses to speed of decision:
- "The first 3 people get a 1:1 strategy session"
- "Join by Friday and get the done-for-you setup"
- "The first 5 get [high-value bonus]"

This rewards fast buyers (who are also the best implementers and most likely to upgrade).

## Sell Past the Close

Once they say yes, use urgency to accelerate onboarding:
- "Great! Our kickoff is Tuesday — I'll send you the prep materials tonight so you're ready"
- "Perfect. The first thing we're going to do is [specific next step] this week"

This reduces buyer's remorse and gets them into motion immediately.

## The Big Rule: No Fake Anything

**Never use fake scarcity.** Never use fake urgency. Never use countdown timers that reset. Never claim "only 3 spots left" when you have unlimited capacity.

Use what's REAL:
- Your actual capacity limits
- Your actual dates and deadlines
- Your actual upcoming events and trainings
- Your actual current client load

If it's real, it's leverage. If it's fake, it's manipulation — and people can smell it.

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Reason to Do It Now
If there's no urgency or scarcity, every prospect will say "let me think about it" and disappear. Always have a reason to act NOW.

### Mistake 2: Thinking It's a Marketing Trick
Scarcity and urgency aren't tricks. They're business realities. You genuinely can only serve X clients. Your kickoff genuinely is next Tuesday. You're just making the prospect aware of facts they need to know.

### Mistake 3: Only Using One Lever
Use BOTH scarcity (limited spots) AND urgency (limited time) together. They compound each other.
