# The Risk Reversers™ (Guarantee)

## Position on the Seesaw
The Guarantee sits on the **RISK** side. It answers the question: "The promise sounds great, but what if it doesn't work?"

Promise + Guarantee are the two biggest needle movers. Get these right and the offer works.

## The Core Principle
Our job is to make it **safe to start**. People have been burned before. The market is noisy. Prospects are skeptical. We need to insert **conversion confidence** — the confidence they need to commit.

## Risk Removal vs. Risk Reversal

**Risk Removal**: "If it doesn't work, I'll give you your money back."
**Risk Reversal**: "If it doesn't work, I'll give you your money back PLUS $1,000 to spend on another coach."

Risk reversal is more powerful — the prospect is BETTER OFF even if it fails.

## The Horse Story

A man wants to buy a horse for his daughter. Two sellers:

**Seller 1**: "My horse is perfect. $1,500. Take it or leave it." → Man says "I'll think about it."

**Seller 2**: "I think my horse would be perfect, but of course I'd say that — it's my horse. How about this: my son will bring the horse to your stable. Your daughter rides it every day for a week. My son comes every morning with fresh oats and a broom. At the end of the week, either they're a match made in heaven and I'll ask to be paid — or it's not right, I take the horse back, you don't owe me a cent."

Which horse gets bought? Seller 2. Every time. Because there's no risk to getting started.

## Guarantee Types

### 1. Love It or Leave It (Time-Based)
- 30-day love it or leave it
- 60-day love it or leave it
- 120-day "one full cycle" love it or leave it

**Danny's script**: "We've known each other for 45 minutes. Right now you seem like a really good guy. But we haven't worked together yet. That's why we've got a 30-day love it or leave it guarantee. Anytime in the first 30 days, if you feel like I'm not the right guy, or I feel like this isn't the right fit — we tear up the agreement and walk away as friends. After 30 days, we're in for a year. Does that sound fair? That way there's no risk to getting started."

### 2. Outcome-Based (Conditional)
- "You'll add $300K in extra profit every year, provided you: don't miss a project, set a goal and hit it, commit to 3 events per year" (Rob Nixon)
- "$10K in 10 weeks — or I work with you for free until you do"
- "If you do everything we outline and it doesn't work, I'll pause your payments and give you extra 1:1 support"

### 3. Buy-It-Back (Risk Reversal)
- "I'll buy it back from you at full price — and give you $10 for a plate of tacos"
- "Full refund plus $1,000 to spend on another coach"

### 4. No Payments Upfront
- Boardroom example: "No payments for the first 2 months. After that, 10 payments of $4,800/month."
- Removes both money AND time risk simultaneously

### 5. Deliberate No-Guarantee
- "There are no guarantees in life. Life rewards winners."
- Completely valid IF it's deliberate (by design, not by default) and you have a story to back it up

## Best Practice Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| Reward good behaviour | Guarantee activates when they DO the work |
| Action based | Tied to specific, simple actions (1-3 things) |
| No questions asked | For low-price offers, make it friction-free |
| Make it safe to start | The guarantee's #1 job |
| By design, not by default | Deliberately choose your guarantee positioning |

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: No Guarantee at All (By Default)
If you haven't thought about your guarantee, that's a red. Even choosing "no guarantee" is fine — but it must be a deliberate decision.

### Mistake 2: Weasel Clauses
If your guarantee reads like something from an insurance company, kill it. If a lawyer had anything to do with it, kill it. No "provided you posted at exactly 11:59 when the algorithm peaked and your video was more than 2 minutes."

**The Test**: Can you say it in 10 seconds in a way that feels obvious, natural, and fair? If yes, you're good. If it needs a paragraph of fine print, start over.

### Mistake 3: "No Skin in the Game" Fear
The best prospects DON'T need a guarantee. They're focused on winning. The guarantee just removes the last bit of friction for good clients who are slightly hesitant. The tiny percentage who might abuse it is far outweighed by the extra clients you'll sign.

### Mistake 4: Not Putting It Up Front
Most coaches would actually refund or give extra support if things went wrong. They just don't SAY it upfront. Put what you'd do anyway into the offer so prospects know before they buy.

## The Key Question
"What happens if they do everything you say, exactly the way you outline it, and it still doesn't work? What would you ACTUALLY do?"

Whatever you answer — that IS your guarantee. You're just making it explicit.
