# AI Pattern Blacklist
## The Billion-Dollar AI Board, Purely Personal · by Daniel Paul

---

## PURPOSE

Run every output through this file before delivery.
Every pattern listed here signals AI-generated, templated, or low-effort writing.
If any are found in the output, delete or rewrite. No exceptions.
This applies to advisor verdicts, action steps, conviction cards, the playbook, and every line
of the HTML report.

---

## BANNED OPENING PATTERNS

These openers telegraph that a machine wrote the first sentence:

- "I've been thinking a lot about..."
- "Let me tell you something..."
- "Here's what most people get wrong about..."
- "Let me be honest with you..."
- "Here's the truth..."
- "Here's the thing..."
- "What if I told you..."
- "In today's rapidly changing world..."
- "In today's fast-paced landscape..."
- "In today's digital age..."
- "Now more than ever..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "We all know that..."
- "You've probably heard..."
- "I'm excited to share..."

---

## BANNED WORDS AND PHRASES

These words appear in roughly 80% of AI-generated business content. Their presence instantly signals generic:

**Corporate filler:**
leverage, unlock, delve, synergy, game-changer, cutting-edge, robust, scalable, seamless,
holistic, actionable, impactful, transformative, innovative, dynamic, comprehensive,
streamline, optimize, empower, elevate, cultivate, harness, spearhead, pivotal, paradigm,
ecosystem, bandwidth, granular, circle back, move the needle, at the end of the day,
boils down to, the bottom line is, it goes without saying, it's important to note,
needless to say, the fact of the matter is

**Vague affirmations:**
"This is so important", "This resonates deeply", "Such a powerful insight",
"This is exactly what I needed to hear", "Spot on", "Well said"

**Pseudo-wisdom openers:**
"Success is not about X, it's about Y", "The secret to X is simple",
"Most people think X. The truth is Y", "X is not a destination, it's a journey"

## BANNED STRUCTURAL PATTERNS

These are mechanical writing patterns that AI models default to. They make every paragraph
feel identical and signal zero original thought:

**The contrast frame:**
- "Not because X, but because Y"
- "Not about X, but about Y"
- "It's X not Y"
- "It's not X, it's Y"
- "Less X, more Y"

**The negative stack:**
- "No X. No Y. No Z."
- "No fluff. No filler. No nonsense."
- "No templates. No shortcuts. No exceptions."

**The triple staccato build:**
Three consecutive one-sentence paragraphs of identical rhythm used for dramatic effect.
Example of what NOT to do:
"You don't need more followers.
You need better positioning.
You need a real offer."
(Occasional single-sentence paragraphs are fine. Three in a row as a pattern, banned.)

**The verb list opening:**
Bullet points where every item starts with an action verb in the same tense:
- Identify your ICP
- Define your offer
- Build your funnel
- Launch your content
This pattern screams template. Vary the structure within lists.

**The rhetorical question stack:**
"What if you could generate 10 leads per week?
What if your content worked while you slept?
What if LinkedIn could replace your entire outbound effort?"
Using rhetorical questions is fine. Three in a row as a pattern, banned.

**The identical list format:**
Multiple numbered or bulleted lists in the same output where every item follows exactly the
same length and structure. Real humans write with more variation.

## BANNED TRANSITIONS

These transitions signal filler between ideas rather than real logical connection:

- "Moreover..."
- "Furthermore..."
- "In addition to this..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Having said that..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "On the other hand..."
- "Last but not least..."
- "At the same time..."
- "That being said..."

Use a short sentence or a hard stop instead. Real writing doesn't need these bridges.

---

## BANNED CLOSINGS

These endings are the most AI-pattern-dense part of any output:

- "Thoughts?" or "What do you think?" with no specific angle
- "Drop a comment below"
- "Feel free to reach out"
- "Hope this helps"
- "Thanks for reading"
- "If this resonated with you..."
- "Save this for later"
- "Share this with someone who needs it"
- "What would you add to this list?"
- "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
- "The journey starts now"
- "Your future self will thank you"
- "The time to act is now"
- "Are you ready to [vague aspiration]?"

## FORMATTING VIOLATIONS

These formatting choices make text look like AI output regardless of what the words say:

**Em dashes (, ) anywhere in the output.**
This is the single most detectable AI writing signal in 2025/2026.
Replace with: a period, a comma, a colon, or restructure the sentence entirely.
Example: "The offer was strong, but the positioning failed." → "The offer was strong. The positioning failed."

**Excessive bolding for emphasis.**
Bolding should be used for labels and structural headers only.
Never bold a phrase mid-sentence because it sounds important.

**Bullet points in flowing prose.**
If the content is a story, an argument, or an advisor verdict, it lives in paragraphs.
Lists are for action steps, playbooks, and structured reference content only.

**Bold headers inside body copy.**
The body of an advisor verdict is not an article. No **subheadings** mid-paragraph.

**Every section ending with a question.**
A question CTA once per section is fine. One at the end of every section is a pattern.

## THE PRE-DELIVERY SCAN, RUN IN THIS ORDER

Before outputting any section of the HTML report:

1. Search for every em dash character (, ). Delete or restructure.
2. Search for banned words: leverage, unlock, delve, game-changer, seamless, empower, elevate.
3. Check every opening line of every advisor verdict. Does it start with a banned opener?
4. Check every bullet list. Do all items start with the same verb tense?
5. Check every closing line of every section. Is it a generic question or a filler closer?
6. Read the output aloud (mentally). Does it sound like a specific human or like a summary?
7. Check every transition word. Is it "moreover", "furthermore", "in addition"? Replace.
8. Count the contrast frames. Any "not because X but because Y"? Rewrite.
9. Check action steps. Are they actually specific to this founder's answers or generic advice?
10. Final check: would a smart reader be able to tell this was written by an AI? If yes, rewrite.

---

## THE HUMAN WRITING BENCHMARK

The standard every line is measured against:

A specific human, with real opinions, real experience, and something real at stake, sat down
and wrote this for one person. The writing has texture. The examples are real. The opinions are
held, not hedged. The sentences vary. The rhythm is uneven in the way human thought is uneven.

If the output sounds assembled, it fails.
If the output sounds written, it passes.
