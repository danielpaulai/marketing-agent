FLOW 1

Get measured: extract your Voice DNA

Start a new session in Claude Code and choose a folder for your agent to live in. Make a new one called my-marketing-agent. This folder is your agent's home, everything it learns lives here.

What to gather first (10 minutes of copy-paste)

  • 5 or more posts you wrote yourself (10 to 20 is better): LinkedIn, X, Instagram captions, newsletters. Your words, not a ghostwriter's.
  • One voice note or call transcript, at least 90 seconds of you talking. A meeting transcript works. This captures your real spoken rhythm, it is the highest-value input.
  • 3 to 10 DM or email replies to real clients. This is your plain explaining voice.
  • Brand basics: one accent color (hex code), light or dark preference, your name, a one-line tagline.

Minimum to proceed: 5 posts or 500 total words. Below that the skill will tell you the samples are too thin and ask for more. Missing the transcript? It proceeds anyway and marks that layer to regenerate later. The messier and more human your samples, the better the extraction.

The prompt

Extract my voice. Here is my raw material: [paste your 5+ posts, your transcript, and a few client replies]. My brand accent color is [hex or "pick one for me"], my name is [name], and my one-line tagline is [tagline].

What the skill actually does (so you know it worked)

  • Analyzes 7 layers with counted evidence, not vague adjectives: your conversational markers and their frequency, sentence patterns (short/medium/long percentages and your rhythm), vocabulary you use often, words you NEVER use, signature phrases (transitions, emphasis, closers), your beliefs and themes, your energy and role, and your format habits.
  • Saves VOICE-DNA.md in your folder with 9 sections, including your brand (section 8) and your business (section 9: what you sell, to whom, the result, your content pillars). Sections 8 and 9 are what power the visual skills and the daily idea engine.
  • Proves it immediately: writes one short sample post using only the DNA file and scores it on the Invisibility Diagnostic (Voice, POV, Specificity, Asking). You should see a 4/4.
  • Builds your DNA profile, an animated page with your stats counting up, your most-used words, your banned list, your rhythm bars, and the validation badge. Screenshot it, that is your voice, captured.

FLOW 2A · WRITE-POST

Write a post, in full detail

The prompt

Write me a post about [your topic]. Raw material: [a story, a result, a belief, or a lesson, in your own rough words].

What happens, beat by beat:

  • The 5-hook gate comes first. Five hooks, five psychological types (Result Lead, Confession, Contrarian Challenge, Curiosity Gap, Pattern Interrupt), each under 12 words. Reply with a number, or say "use the strongest one".
  • The post is written in YOUR voice: your chosen hook unchanged, a rehook line that deepens the tension, body paragraphs of 3 lines max, exactly one CTA, and a P.S. as the most human line. 1,300 to 1,500 characters. It reads VOICE-DNA.md before writing a word and kills every pattern on the AI blacklist.
  • It scores itself on the Invisibility Diagnostic and fixes anything below 4/4 before you see it.
  • You get 3 pinned comment options (a bonus insight, an audience question, a soft plug) and an animated LinkedIn-style preview card with the score badge.

FLOW 2B · BUILD-CAROUSEL

Build a carousel

The prompt

Make me a LinkedIn carousel about [topic], 6 slides. Use my brand from VOICE-DNA.md.

The flow:

  • 1. The deck comes first: cover hook, body slides, CTA slide, plus a caption. You approve or tweak before anything else happens. Say "slide 3 needs a stronger title" and it fixes just that.
  • 2. The designed preview: every slide rendered as styled cards in your palette, so you SEE the whole carousel before spending a single image generation.
  • 3. One image prompt per slide, numbered, in copyable blocks, each repeating the shared template so the set looks like ONE cohesive deck.
  • 4. You produce it: paste each prompt into your image tool at 1088x1360 (4:5 portrait), then combine the finished images into a single PDF and post it on LinkedIn as a document.

Instagram instead? Say so, it drops the LinkedIn header, keeps it cleaner, square or 4:5.

FLOW 2C · IMAGE-POST

One image post

The prompt

Image post about [concept]. Platform: [LinkedIn or Instagram]. Text that must appear on the image: [your headline or stat, verbatim].
  • You get ONE complete, self-contained image prompt: exact size (1088x1360 LinkedIn portrait, 1088x1088 Instagram square), your brand colors, the text rendered verbatim with key words in your accent, and composition notes.
  • Paste it into any image model you already use (gpt-image, Midjourney, DALL-E). Regenerate with small wording tweaks to steer.
  • Ask for "a quick layout mock first" if you want to sanity-check the design before generating, or "3 headline variations" for options.

FLOW 2D · BUILD-OFFER

Build your offer (and watch the score climb)

The prompt

Build my offer.

What happens: a guided interview through 6 milestones, one at a time: your diagnosis (what you sell now and how it converts), your 10-word promise, your 10-second guarantee, your bonus stack, your payment plan, and your real reason to act now. Answer honestly, it coaches weak answers in a sentence or two.

Then the Diamond Score, out of 100: every facet scored with points and a traffic light (Promise and Guarantee are worth 30 each, they count double), one honest line per facet quoting your own words, and the single highest-leverage fix. Then the rescore loop: fix the weakest lever together, rescore, watch the number move. "54 → 78. That one fix bought you 24 points." Keep going to 90+.

The payoff: a complete, animated offer document with the Diamond on the cover, every facet colored, your score counting up, and your journey line (54 → 78 → 91) rendered on it.

Already have an offer? Score it first

Score my offer on the Diamond: [paste your current offer exactly as you say it on a call, unpolished].

FLOW 3

Connect Apify: give your agent research arms

Your skills work with zero connectors. But connected to Apify, your agent can research what is trending in your niche TODAY and write from live data instead of memory. This is the upgrade you watched on stage. Do it at home, it takes 10 minutes.

Set it up (once)

  • 1. Create a free account at apify.com (the free plan includes monthly credits, no card needed).
  • 2. In Claude, open Settings › Connectors › Add custom connector and paste: https://mcp.apify.com
  • 3. A browser window opens, log in to Apify and approve. That is the whole connection.
  • 4. Test it: in a new session say "Using Apify, find 3 things trending in [your niche] this week." If results come back, you are wired.

The Master Prompt (copy, paste, run)

This is the full daily engine in one prompt: live research, one chosen idea, a scored post in your voice, the image prompt, all saved to a file. Run it any morning, or hand it to your routine below.

You are my Marketing Agent, my daily content engine. Run the full production cycle now, start to finish, and do not stop to ask me questions unless a rule below forces you to.

MY CONTEXT, READ FIRST
Read VOICE-DNA.md in this folder, top to bottom, before anything else. It is the only source of truth for my voice (markers, rhythm, vocabulary, banned words, signature phrases, format habits), my brand (section 8), and my business (section 9: what I sell, who I sell to, the result I deliver, my content pillars). Every sentence you produce must obey it. Never invent facts, results, numbers, client names, or stories about me. If a specific is missing and needed, use a clearly marked placeholder like [X%] and flag it at the end.

STEP 1, RESEARCH (Apify)
Using the Apify connector, research what is moving for my audience right now:
a) What my ideal customers (section 9) are discussing this week: pains, debates, questions, news.
b) What is trending inside my content pillars in the last 7 days.
Bring back exactly 3 candidate ideas. For each: the insight in one line, why it matters right now, where you found it, and which pillar it serves.
If Apify is not connected or a search fails, do not stop. Fall back to your own knowledge of my pillars and label each idea [NOT LIVE RESEARCH].

STEP 2, CHOOSE
Pick the strongest of the 3 using: relevance to my ideal customer, freshness, and my right to win the topic (my authority). State your pick and your reason in one line.

STEP 3, WRITE (use the write-post skill standards)
First write 5 hooks, each a different type: Result Lead, Confession, Contrarian Challenge, Curiosity Gap, Pattern Interrupt. Each under 12 words. Select the strongest yourself and say why in one sentence.
Then write the full post in MY voice: the chosen hook unchanged, a rehook line that deepens the tension, body paragraphs of 3 lines maximum, exactly one CTA, and a P.S. as the most human line. Length 1,300 to 1,500 characters. Kill every pattern on the AI blacklist. Never use an em dash or en dash anywhere.

STEP 4, SCORE
Score the post on the Invisibility Diagnostic: Voice, POV, Specificity, Asking. If it is below 4/4, fix the failing dimension and rescore before continuing. Show the final score.

STEP 5, THE VISUAL
Write one complete, self-contained image-generation prompt for this post using my brand colors and platform size, ready to paste into any image tool.

STEP 6, DELIVER
Save everything into today-post.md in this folder: today's date, the idea and its source, the final post, the diagnostic score, 3 pinned comment options (one bonus insight, one audience question, one soft plug), and the image prompt. Then show me the post text in a copyable block and finish with one line: the idea you would run tomorrow.

Printed length is about 500 words. It chains research, choice, writing, scoring, visual, and delivery into one run. Change nothing on first use, tune it only after you have run it three times.

FLOW 4

The routine: your standing order

This is Level 3, and you have two ways to set it. Pick one.

Option A, the remote routine (Cowork, the easy one)

Cowork is the no-terminal face of the same engine, in the same app: in the message box, select Cowork instead of Chat. Your plugin's skills work there too. Then type /schedule and describe the daily task (use the routine prompt below). Cowork scheduled tasks run on Anthropic's servers, your laptop can be closed and asleep. This is the true "works while you sleep" option.

Cowork uses more of your plan's usage than chat, one daily post is fine. Keep the desktop app for anything that needs your local folder.

Option B, the local routine (Claude Code)

The routine runs on your computer, so it needs the conditions below to be true, check all five:

  • Your computer is on and awake at the scheduled time (plugged in, lid open or sleep disabled for that hour).
  • Claude Code can run, you are signed in and the app opens without asking you anything.
  • Always the same folder. The routine must run inside my-marketing-agent, where VOICE-DNA.md lives.
  • VOICE-DNA.md exists and is current. The agent cannot sound like you while you sleep without the ledger.
  • Say the timezone. "7am" means your local 7am, name it when you set the routine.

Set it (paste in your agent folder, or into /schedule in Cowork)

Create a routine that runs locally on this computer every morning at 7am my time (timezone: [your timezone]) inside this folder. Each run: read VOICE-DNA.md, then execute the Master Prompt saved in master-prompt.md from research through delivery, and leave the finished post, score, pinned comments, and image prompt in today-post.md. If anything fails, write what happened into today-post.md instead of failing silently. Confirm the schedule back to me.

Save the Master Prompt from Flow 3 as master-prompt.md in your agent folder first, then the routine above can call it every morning. Ask your agent: "save the master prompt I just gave you as master-prompt.md".

Not ready to schedule? The manual morning

Same power, one action: open Claude Code in your folder each morning with coffee and say "run my master prompt". Do that for three days. When you trust the output, set the routine and stop showing up.
